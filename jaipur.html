Jaipur, Rajasthan

The Pink City - Where Royalty Meets Culture

28°C • Sunny

Overview

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to the distinctive color of its buildings. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it's a city of majestic forts, palaces, vibrant bazaars, and rich cultural heritage. Jaipur forms part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, architecture, and vibrant culture.

How to Reach Jaipur

30 min from airport

By Air

  • Jaipur International Airport (JAI) - 13km from city center
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad
  • International connections from Dubai, Muscat, Bangkok
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, Vistara
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹300-500 to city center)
4-5 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Jaipur Junction (JP) - main railway station
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Shatabdi Express - 4.5 hrs)
  • Luxury options: Palace on Wheels, Royal Rajasthan
  • Other important trains: Rajdhani Express, Duronto
  • Auto-rickshaws available outside station
5-6 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Excellent NH48 highway connectivity
  • Delhi-Jaipur via Gurugram, Behror, Kotputli
  • Volvo/AC buses from Delhi (₹500-800 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹3000-4000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Rajasthan Roadways, Zingbus

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Amber Fort

Majestic hilltop fort-palace with stunning mirror work, frescoes, and panoramic views of Maota Lake. Don't miss the light show in evenings.

Jantar Mantar

Jaipur's Royal Cuisine

Dal Baati Churma

Rajasthan's signature dish - baked wheat balls (baati) served with lentils (dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (churma).

Best at: Laxmi Misthan Bhandar

Laal Maas

Fiery mutton curry made with Mathania red chilies, garlic and yogurt. Traditionally cooked in ghee.

Best at: 1135 AD (Amber Fort)

Ghewar

Disc-shaped sweet made from flour and soaked in sugar syrup. Often topped with rabri (thickened milk).

Best at: Rawat Mishthan Bhandar

Pyaaz Kachori

Flaky deep-fried pastry stuffed with spicy onion mixture. Served with tamarind and coriander chutneys.

Best at: Samrat Restaurant

Ker Sangri

Traditional Rajasthani dish made with desert beans (sangri) and capers (ker), cooked in spices and yogurt.

Best at: Chokhi Dhani

Mawa Kachori

Sweet version of kachori stuffed with mawa (reduced milk), nuts and cardamom, deep-fried and dipped in syrup.

Best at: JMB Sweet House

Best Time to Visit

Winter

8°C - 22°C
Oct - Feb
Ideal for sightseeing with pleasant days and cool nights. Peak tourist season with cultural festivals.

Spring

20°C - 35°C
Mar - Apr
Warm days but manageable. Good for photography with clear skies. Hotel rates drop slightly.

Summer

25°C - 45°C
May - Jun
Extremely hot, not ideal for outdoor activities. Early mornings and evenings are better for sightseeing.

Monsoon

24°C - 36°C
Jul - Sep
Moderate rainfall brings relief from heat. Lush green landscapes but some outdoor activities may be limited.
