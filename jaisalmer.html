Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

The Golden City - Where the Desert Meets Royalty

32°C • Sunny

Overview

Jaisalmer, the golden jewel of Rajasthan, rises like a mirage from the sands of the Thar Desert. Known as the "Golden City" for its yellow sandstone architecture that glows in the sunlight, Jaisalmer is a living fort city with a rich history dating back to 1156 AD when it was founded by Maharawal Jaisal. The city's stunning havelis, ancient temples, and the massive Jaisalmer Fort create a magical atmosphere that transports visitors to another era. As a key stop on the ancient camel caravan routes, Jaisalmer's culture reflects a unique blend of Rajput and Islamic influences.

How to Reach Jaisalmer

30 min from airport

By Air

  • Jaisalmer Airport (JSA) - 17km from city
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Jaipur, Mumbai
  • International connections via Delhi/Mumbai
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, SpiceJet
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹500-800 to city center)
16-18 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Jaisalmer Railway Station (JSM)
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Delhi-Jaisalmer Express - 18 hrs)
  • Luxury option: Palace on Wheels
  • Other important trains: Jodhpur-Jaisalmer Express
  • Auto-rickshaws & taxis available outside station
12-14 hrs from Jaipur

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH11 and NH125
  • Jaipur-Jaisalmer via Jodhpur, Pokhran
  • Volvo/AC buses from Jaipur (₹800-1200 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹7000-9000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Rajasthan Roadways, RSRTC

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Jaisalmer Fort (Sonar Quila)

One of the world's few living forts, this golden sandstone structure houses shops, hotels, and homes within its walls. A UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning views of the city.

4.8 (5,210 reviews)
Free Entry
Iconic

Patwon Ki Haveli

Cluster of five elaborate mansions with intricate carvings and latticework. The most famous haveli in Jaisalmer, showcasing the wealth of 18th century merchants.

Sam Sand Dunes

Classic desert landscape 40km from Jaisalmer, perfect for camel safaris and experiencing Rajasthani folk culture. Stunning sunsets and overnight camping available.

Salim Singh Ki Haveli

300-year-old mansion with a distinctive peacock-shaped roof and beautiful jharokhas (balconies). Named after Jaisalmer's prime minister who built it.

Nathmal Ki Haveli

19th century haveli built by two brothers, featuring fascinating asymmetrical design and exquisite carvings of animals, flowers and miniature soldiers.

Bada Bagh

Jaisalmer's Desert Cuisine

Ker Sangri

Traditional Rajasthani dish made with desert beans (sangri) and berries (ker), cooked with spices and yogurt.

Best at: The Trio Restaurant

Laal Maas

Fiery mutton curry made with Mathania red chilies, garlic and yogurt. Traditionally cooked in ghee.

Best at: Saffron Restaurant

Ghotua Ladoo

Traditional sweet made from gram flour, ghee and sugar, often served during festivals.

Best at: Shahi Sweet Home

Pyaaz Kachori

Flaky pastry stuffed with spiced onion mixture, deep-fried and served with tangy chutneys.

Best at: Kachori Wala near Patwa Haveli

Dal Baati Churma

Rajasthan's signature dish - baked wheat balls (baati) served with lentils (dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (churma).

Best at: Desert Boy's Dhani

Gatte Ki Sabzi

Gram flour dumplings cooked in spiced yogurt gravy, a staple in Rajasthani households.

Best at: The Kalinga Restaurant

Best Time to Visit

Winter

8°C - 24°C
Oct - Feb
Ideal time to visit with pleasant days and cool nights. Desert Festival in February is a major attraction.

Spring

20°C - 35°C
Mar - Apr
Warm days but manageable. Great for photography with clear skies. Holi celebrations are vibrant.

Summer

25°C - 48°C
May - Jul
Extremely hot with daytime temperatures often exceeding 45°C. Not recommended for outdoor activities.

Monsoon

25°C - 38°C
Aug - Sep
Minimal rainfall but high humidity. Some relief from heat but still quite warm during the day.
