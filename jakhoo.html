Jakhoo Temple

Shimla's Sacred Peak with Panoramic Himalayan Views

2,455m • Spiritual Retreat

Overview

Perched atop Jakhoo Hill, the highest point in Shimla at 2,455 meters, Jakhoo Temple is an ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Hanuman. The temple is famous for its towering 108-foot orange-colored Hanuman statue, visible from many parts of Shimla. According to legend, this is where Lord Hanuman rested during his journey to the Himalayas to bring the Sanjeevani herb for Lakshmana in the Ramayana. The temple offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Shivalik ranges and the town below. The serene atmosphere, combined with playful monkeys and the scent of cedar forests, makes it a must-visit spiritual and scenic destination in Shimla.

Temple Details

History & Significance

  • Ancient temple mentioned in Hindu scriptures
  • Believed to be 2,500+ years old
  • Current structure built in 19th century
  • Giant Hanuman statue installed in 2010
  • Considered one of Shimla's most sacred sites

How to Reach

  • Ropeway: 5-min ride from near Christ Church (₹300 round trip)
  • Trek: 30-45 min steep hike through deodar forest
  • Taxi: Available up to parking area (1km walk from there)
  • Distance from Mall Road: 2.5km
  • GPS Coordinates: 31.1039° N, 77.1716° E

Temple Timings & Rituals

  • Opening Hours: 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily
  • Aarti Timings: 6:00 AM and 6:00 PM
  • Special pujas on Tuesdays and Saturdays
  • Annual festival during Hanuman Jayanti
  • Photography allowed (no flash inside sanctum)

Visitor Tips

Monkey Safety

The temple area has many monkeys. Avoid carrying food openly, wearing shiny accessories, or making direct eye contact with them. Temple staff can help if monkeys become aggressive.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (6-8 AM) offers peaceful darshan and beautiful sunrise views. Evenings are crowded but provide spectacular sunset vistas over the Himalayas.

What to Wear

Dress modestly as it's a religious site. Comfortable walking shoes are essential, especially if trekking up the hill. Carry a light jacket as it can be windy at the top.

Photography Tips

The golden hour (just after sunrise or before sunset) provides the best lighting for photos of the statue. Wide-angle lenses work best to capture the entire statue.

Trekking Advice

The forest trail is steep but well-marked. Carry water and take breaks. Elderly visitors or those with mobility issues should consider the ropeway instead.

Nearby Facilities

Small shops sell prasad, water, and souvenirs. Limited toilet facilities available. No restaurants at the top, so eat before visiting or carry snacks.
