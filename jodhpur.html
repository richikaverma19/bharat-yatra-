Jodhpur, Rajasthan

The Blue City - Gateway to the Thar Desert

32°C • Sunny

Overview

Jodhpur, the second largest city in Rajasthan, is famously known as the "Blue City" for the blue-painted houses around the Mehrangarh Fort. Founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, it was the capital of the former princely state of Marwar. Jodhpur is a mesmerizing blend of history, culture, and vibrant colors, with its majestic forts, bustling bazaars, and the vast Thar Desert at its doorstep.

How to Reach Jodhpur

20 min from airport

By Air

  • Jodhpur Airport (JDH) - 5km from city center
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Udaipur
  • International connections via Delhi/Mumbai
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹200-400 to city center)
5-6 hrs from Jaipur

By Train

  • Jodhpur Junction (JU) - main railway station
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Mandore Express - 12 hrs)
  • Luxury options: Palace on Wheels, Royal Rajasthan
  • Other important trains: Suryanagari Express
  • Auto-rickshaws available outside station
6-7 hrs from Jaipur

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH62 and NH58
  • Jaipur-Jodhpur via Ajmer, Beawar, Pali
  • Volvo/AC buses from Jaipur (₹600-900 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹4000-5000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Rajasthan Roadways, Zingbus

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Mehrangarh Fort

One of India's largest forts, towering 410 feet above the city with magnificent palaces, intricate carvings, and panoramic views of the blue city.

Umaid Bhawan Palace

One of the world's largest private residences with parts converted into a luxury hotel and museum showcasing royal artifacts and vintage cars.

Heritage Site

Jaswant Thada

Beautiful white marble memorial built in 1899 in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II, with intricate lattice work and peaceful gardens.

Mandore Gardens

Ancient capital of Marwar with beautiful gardens, royal cenotaphs, and a hall of heroes with huge statues of Rajput warriors.

Sardar Market

Vibrant market around Clock Tower with shops selling spices, textiles, handicrafts, and the famous Jodhpuri mojris (traditional shoes).

Jodhpur's Royal Cuisine

Dal Bati Churma

Rajasthan's signature dish - baked wheat balls (baati) served with lentils (dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (churma).

Best at: Gypsy Restaurant

Laal Maas

Fiery mutton curry made with Mathania red chilies, garlic and yogurt. Traditionally cooked in ghee.

Best at: Indique Restaurant

Mawa Kachori

Sweet version of kachori stuffed with mawa (reduced milk), nuts and cardamom, deep-fried and dipped in syrup.

Best at: Janta Sweet Home

Makhaniya Lassi

Creamy yogurt drink topped with thick malai (cream), saffron and dry fruits. A Jodhpur specialty.

Best at: Shri Mishrilal Hotel

Gatte ki Sabzi

Gram flour dumplings cooked in spicy yogurt gravy, a Rajasthani staple often served with bajra roti.

Best at: On The Rocks

Malpua

Sweet pancake made from flour, milk and sugar, fried in ghee and served with rabri (thickened milk).

Best at: Jodhpur Sweets

Best Time to Visit

Winter

10°C - 25°C
Oct - Feb
Perfect weather for exploring with cool mornings and pleasant days. Peak season with cultural festivals.

Spring

22°C - 38°C
Mar - Apr
Warm days but manageable. Good for photography with clear skies. Hotel rates begin to drop.

Summer

28°C - 48°C
May - Jun
Extremely hot, not ideal for outdoor activities. Early mornings and late evenings are better.

Monsoon

26°C - 38°C
Jul - Sep
Scant rainfall brings some relief from heat. Lush landscapes but humidity can be high.
