Kerala, God's Own Country

Where Backwaters, Beaches, and Lush Greenery Create Paradise

28°C • Partly Cloudy

Overview

Kerala, nestled on India's tropical Malabar Coast, is a land of extraordinary beauty and cultural richness. Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala is famous for its palm-lined beaches, tranquil backwaters, lush hill stations, and vibrant traditions. From the serene houseboat cruises in Alleppey to the misty tea plantations of Munnar, from the historic port city of Kochi to the wildlife-rich forests of Periyar, Kerala offers diverse experiences. The state's unique culture, shaped by its spice trade history, is reflected in its distinctive cuisine, classical Kathakali dance, and ancient Ayurvedic practices.

How to Reach Kerala

30 min from airport

By Air

  • Cochin International Airport (COK) - Main gateway
  • Other airports: Trivandrum (TRV), Calicut (CCJ)
  • Direct flights from major Indian cities & international hubs
  • Major airlines: Air India, IndiGo, Emirates, Qatar
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹800-1500 to city center)
48-52 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Major stations: Ernakulam, Trivandrum, Kozhikode
  • Rajdhani Express from Delhi (48 hrs)
  • Other important trains: Kerala Express, Trivandrum Mail
  • Luxury option: Maharajas' Express
  • Auto-rickshaws & taxis available outside stations
12-14 hrs from Bangalore

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH66, NH544, NH966
  • Bangalore to Kochi via Salem, Coimbatore
  • Volvo/AC buses from major cities (₹1000-1800 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹8-12 per km)
  • Major bus operators: KSRTC, private operators

Kerala's Flavorful Cuisine

Sadya

Traditional vegetarian feast served on banana leaf with 20+ dishes including sambar, avial, thoran, and payasam, typically during festivals.

Best at: Grand Hotel, Kochi

Kerala Chicken Curry

Aromatic chicken cooked in coconut milk with roasted spices, curry leaves, and shallots, served with appam or parotta.

Best at: Paragon Restaurant, Kozhikode

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaf and grilled, a signature dish of Kerala backwaters.

Best at: Harbour Restaurant, Alleppey

Puttu & Kadala

Steamed rice cakes layered with coconut served with black chickpea curry, a popular breakfast dish.

Best at: Aryaas, Trivandrum

Palada Payasam

Rich rice pudding made with milk, sugar, and rice ada, traditionally served during Onam festival.

Best at: Grand Sweets, Kochi

Malabar Biryani

Fragrant rice dish cooked with chicken or mutton, spices, and fried onions, distinct from other biryanis.

Best at: Zains, Malappuram

Best Time to Visit

Winter

23°C - 32°C
Sep - Feb
Pleasant weather perfect for backwaters, beaches, and hill stations. Peak tourist season with cultural festivals.

Summer

29°C - 38°C
Mar - May
Hot and humid, but hill stations remain pleasant. Good for Ayurvedic treatments with fewer crowds.

Monsoon

25°C - 30°C
Jun - Aug
Heavy rainfall creates lush landscapes. Ideal for Ayurvedic therapies and experiencing Kerala's greenery.

Shoulder Season

26°C - 34°C
Mar & Sep
Transition periods with moderate weather and fewer tourists. Great for budget travelers.
