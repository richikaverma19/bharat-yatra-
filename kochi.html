Kochi, Queen of Arabian Sea

Where Colonial Heritage Meets Coastal Charm

30°C • Humid

Overview

Kochi (formerly Cochin), the vibrant port city on Kerala's southwest coast, is a fascinating blend of colonial history, bustling trade, and coastal beauty. Known as the "Queen of the Arabian Sea," Kochi has been a trading hub for centuries, attracting Chinese, Arabs, Dutch, Portuguese, and British merchants. Today, it enchants visitors with its historic Fort Kochi area featuring colonial architecture, Chinese fishing nets, spice markets, and art galleries. The city serves as the perfect introduction to Kerala's diverse culture, with its Jewish Synagogue, Portuguese churches, Dutch palaces, and traditional Kathakali dance performances.

How to Reach Kochi

30 min from airport

By Air

  • Cochin International Airport (COK) - 30km northeast
  • India's first solar-powered airport
  • Direct flights from major Indian cities & international hubs
  • Major airlines: Air India, IndiGo, Emirates, Qatar
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹800-1200 to city center)
48-52 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Major stations: Ernakulam Junction, Ernakulam Town
  • Rajdhani Express from Delhi (48 hrs)
  • Other important trains: Kerala Express, Mumbai Express
  • Ferry service available from Ernakulam to Fort Kochi
  • Auto-rickshaws & taxis available outside stations
10-12 hrs from Bangalore

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH66, NH544, NH966
  • Bangalore to Kochi via Salem, Coimbatore
  • Volvo/AC buses from major cities (₹900-1500 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹8-12 per km)
  • Major bus operators: KSRTC, private operators

Must-Visit Attractions in Kochi

Iconic

Chinese Fishing Nets

Iconic cantilevered fishing nets introduced by Chinese traders in the 14th century. Best viewed at sunset when fishermen operate them. A perfect spot for photography.

Fort Kochi

Historic neighborhood with colonial architecture, art galleries, and cafes. Walk through Princess Street to see Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences.

Jewish Synagogue

Built in 1568, this is the oldest active synagogue in Commonwealth nations. Features Chinese hand-painted tiles and Belgian chandeliers.

Kochi's Flavorful Cuisine

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaf and grilled, a signature dish of Kerala backwaters.

Best at: Grand Hotel, Fort Kochi

Kerala Chicken Stew

Mildly spiced chicken cooked in coconut milk with potatoes, onions and aromatic spices, served with appam.

Best at: Kashi Art Cafe

Appam with Stew

Lacy rice-hoppers served with vegetable or meat stew in coconut milk, a classic Kerala breakfast.

Best at: Dal Roti

Kochi Porotta

Flaky, layered flatbread served with beef or chicken curry, a local favorite available at street stalls.

Best at: Kayees Rahmathullah Cafe

Kallummakkaya Fry

Mussels marinated in spices and shallow fried, a coastal delicacy best enjoyed fresh from fishing boats.

Best at: Fort House Restaurant

Puttu & Kadala

Steamed rice cakes layered with coconut served with black chickpea curry, a popular breakfast dish.

Best at: Sree Krishna Cafe

Best Time to Visit Kochi

Winter

23°C - 32°C
Oct - Feb
Pleasant weather perfect for sightseeing. Peak tourist season with cultural festivals like Kochi-Muziris Biennale.

Summer

29°C - 38°C
Mar - May
Hot and humid, but mornings and evenings are bearable. Good for indoor attractions and museum visits.

Monsoon

25°C - 30°C
Jun - Sep
Heavy rainfall creates lush landscapes. Fewer tourists and lower prices, but some outdoor activities may be limited.

Shoulder Season

26°C - 34°C
Mar & Sep
Transition periods with moderate weather and fewer tourists. Great for budget travelers.
