Pushkar, Rajasthan

The Sacred Lake Town - Where Spirituality Meets Culture

28°C • Pleasant

Overview

Pushkar, one of the oldest cities in India, is a sacred town surrounding the holy Pushkar Lake, believed to have been created by Lord Brahma. Known for its vibrant ghats, temples, and the world-famous Pushkar Camel Fair, this spiritual oasis in the Thar Desert offers a unique blend of religious significance and cultural richness. The town's narrow lanes, bustling bazaars, and the serene lake create an atmosphere of timeless charm.

How to Reach Pushkar

30 min from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Jaipur International (JAI) - 150km away
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad
  • International connections via Delhi/Mumbai
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India, Vistara
  • Taxi from Jaipur to Pushkar (₹2500-3500 one way)
30 min from station

By Train

  • Nearest station: Ajmer Junction (AII) - 15km away
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Shatabdi Express - 6 hrs)
  • Other important trains: Ajmer Shatabdi, Delhi-Ajmer Express
  • Auto-rickshaws/buses available from Ajmer to Pushkar
  • Shared taxis also available (₹200-300 per person)
3-4 hrs from Jaipur

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH58 and state highways
  • Jaipur-Pushkar via Kishangarh (145km)
  • Regular buses from Jaipur (₹200-400 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹2000-3000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Rajasthan Roadways, Private Volvos

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

Pushkar Lake

The sacred lake with 52 ghats where pilgrims bathe to wash away sins. Surrounded by hundreds of temples, it's the spiritual heart of Pushkar.

4.8 (5,420 reviews)
Free Entry
Religious

Brahma Temple

One of the very few temples in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma, built in the 14th century with red spire and hamsa (goose) motif.

4.7 (4,810 reviews)
Free Entry

Pushkar Camel Fair

World's largest camel fair held annually in November, transforming Pushkar into a vibrant spectacle of trading, competitions and cultural events.

4.9 (7,890 reviews)
₹100 Entry

Savitri Temple

Pushkar's Spiritual Cuisine

Dal Baati Churma

Rajasthan's signature dish - baked wheat balls (baati) served with lentils (dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (churma).

Best at: Prem Ratan Restaurant

Pyaz Kachori

Flaky deep-fried pastry stuffed with spicy onion mixture, served with tangy tamarind chutney.

Best at: Shri Shankar Kachori Wala

Malpua

Sweet pancake made from flour, milk and sugar, fried in ghee and served with rabri (thickened milk).

Best at: Shri Mishrilal Hotel

Israeli Platter

Hummus, falafel, pita bread and salads - legacy of Israeli backpackers who frequent Pushkar.

Best at: Little Taste of Israel

Gatte ki Sabzi

Gram flour dumplings cooked in spicy yogurt gravy, a Rajasthani staple often served with bajra roti.

Best at: Honey Dew Restaurant

Rabri

Sweet, condensed-milk-based dish made by boiling milk on low heat for a long time until it becomes dense.

Best at: Blue Star Restaurant
