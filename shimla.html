Shimla, Queen of Hills

Where Colonial Charm Meets Himalayan Majesty

15°C • Pleasant

Overview

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a picturesque hill station nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas at an altitude of 2,205 meters. Once the summer capital of British India, Shimla retains its colonial charm with neo-gothic architecture, charming cottages, and the iconic Mall Road. The town offers breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks, lush green valleys, and dense forests of pine, oak, and rhododendron. Shimla's pleasant climate, scenic beauty, and rich history make it one of India's most popular hill stations, attracting visitors year-round for its colonial heritage, adventure activities, and natural splendor.

How to Reach Shimla

1 hr from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Chandigarh (IXC) - 115km away
  • Jubbarhatti Airport (SLV) - 22km from Shimla (limited flights)
  • Major airlines: Air India, IndiGo, SpiceJet
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹2,500-3,500 to Shimla)
  • Helicopter services available from Chandigarh
8-10 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Kalka-Shimla Toy Train (UNESCO World Heritage)
  • Scenic 96km journey through 103 tunnels
  • Overnight trains from Delhi to Kalka
  • Major trains: Shatabdi Express, Himalayan Queen
  • Taxis available from Kalka station
7-8 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH5 and NH205
  • Delhi to Shimla via Chandigarh, Panchkula
  • Volvo/AC buses from Delhi, Chandigarh (₹800-1,200)
  • Private taxi options (₹10-15 per km)
  • Major bus operators: HRTC, private operators

Must-Visit Attractions in Shimla

Iconic

The Mall Road

Shimla's vibrant heart with colonial buildings, shops, cafes, and the iconic Gaiety Theatre. Pedestrian-only zone perfect for leisurely strolls and shopping for local handicrafts.

Shimla's Flavorful Cuisine

Siddu

Traditional Himachali steamed bread made with wheat flour and stuffed with poppy seeds, walnuts or paneer, served with ghee.

Best at: Himachali Rasoi

Chha Gosht

Tender lamb cooked in yogurt-based gravy with aromatic spices, a Himachali specialty often served with rice or roti.

Best at: Ashiana & Goofa

Madra

Creamy chickpea or kidney bean curry cooked in yogurt with aromatic spices, a festive dish in Himachali cuisine.

Best at: Himachal Emporium

Momos

Tibetan-style dumplings stuffed with vegetables or meat, served with fiery red chutney, a Shimla street food staple.

Best at: Tibetan Market stalls

Aktori

Festive buckwheat pancake cake made with layers of sweetened pancakes, a traditional Spiti Valley specialty.

Best at: Wake & Bake

Babru

Himachali version of kachori - deep-fried bread stuffed with black gram paste, served with tamarind chutney.

Best at: Indian Coffee House

Best Time to Visit Shimla

Spring

10°C - 20°C
Mar - Apr
Pleasant weather with blooming rhododendrons. Ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities without extreme cold.

Summer

15°C - 30°C
May - Jun
Peak tourist season with perfect escape from plains' heat. Great for trekking and adventure activities.

Monsoon

13°C - 25°C
Jul - Sep
Lush green landscapes but frequent landslides may disrupt travel. Fewer crowds and lower prices.

Winter

-5°C - 10°C
Dec - Feb
Snow transforms Shimla into winter wonderland. Ideal for snow activities but some roads may close.
