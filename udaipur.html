Udaipur, Rajasthan

The City of Lakes - Venice of the East

26°C • Pleasant

Overview

Udaipur, the jewel of Rajasthan, is a romantic city of lakes, palaces, and gardens. Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, it's known for its picturesque lakes and magnificent Rajput-era palaces. Often called the "Venice of the East," Udaipur's stunning architecture, colorful markets, and serene lakes make it one of India's most beautiful cities. The city served as the historic capital of the Mewar kingdom and retains much of its regal charm.

How to Reach Udaipur

20 min from airport

By Air

  • Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR) - 22km from city
  • Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Hyderabad
  • International connections via Delhi/Mumbai
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹400-600 to city center)
8-9 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Udaipur City Railway Station (UDZ)
  • Direct trains from Delhi (Mewar Express - 12 hrs)
  • Luxury option: Palace on Wheels
  • Other important trains: Chetak Express, Rajasthan Sampark Kranti
  • Auto-rickshaws & taxis available outside station
10-12 hrs from Delhi

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH48 and NH58
  • Delhi-Udaipur via Jaipur, Chittorgarh
  • Volvo/AC buses from Delhi (₹1000-1500 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹6000-8000 one way)
  • Major bus operators: Rajasthan Roadways, Zingbus

Must-Visit Attractions

Top Rated

City Palace

Majestic palace complex on the banks of Lake Pichola, combining Rajasthani and Mughal architecture. Houses museums with royal artifacts and offers stunning lake views.

Lake Palace

Floating white marble palace in the middle of Lake Pichola, now a luxury hotel. Featured in the James Bond film "Octopussy". Best viewed from City Palace or boat ride.

Sunset Point

Sajjangarh (Monsoon Palace)

Hilltop palace offering panoramic views of Udaipur and its lakes. Built to watch monsoon clouds, it's particularly stunning at sunset when the city lights begin to twinkle.

4.5 (4,120 reviews)

Jag Mandir

Island palace on Lake Pichola with beautiful gardens and marble architecture. Served as refuge to Prince Khurram (later Shah Jahan), inspiring the Taj Mahal's design.

4.4 (3,780 reviews)
300 (Boat ride)
Architectural Gem

Jagdish Temple

17th century Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its intricate carvings and Indo-Aryan architecture. Located near City Palace in the old city.

Kumbhalgarh Fort

Udaipur's Royal Cuisine

Dal Baati Churma

Rajasthan's signature dish - baked wheat balls (baati) served with lentils (dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (churma).

Best at: Ambrai Ghat

Laal Maas

Fiery mutton curry made with Mathania red chilies, garlic and yogurt. Traditionally cooked in ghee.

Best at: Upre by 1559 AD

Ghewar

Disc-shaped sweet made from flour and soaked in sugar syrup. Often topped with rabri (thickened milk).

Best at: Jodhpur Misthan Bhandar

Mirchi Bada

Large green chilies stuffed with spiced potato mixture, dipped in gram flour batter and deep-fried.

Best at: Krishna Dal Bati Restro

Mawa Kachori

Sweet version of kachori stuffed with mawa (reduced milk), nuts and cardamom, deep-fried and dipped in syrup.

Best at: JMB Sweet House

Rajasthani Kadhi

Tangy yogurt-based curry thickened with gram flour, tempered with spices and served with steamed rice.

Best at: Natraj Dining Hall

Best Time to Visit

Winter

10°C - 25°C
Oct - Feb
Perfect weather for sightseeing with cool mornings and pleasant days. Peak tourist season with cultural events.

Spring

18°C - 32°C
Mar - Apr
Warm days but manageable. Great for photography with clear skies. Mewar Festival in March/April.

Summer

25°C - 42°C
May - Jun
Hot and dry, not ideal for outdoor activities. Early mornings and evenings are better for sightseeing.

Monsoon

22°C - 35°C
Jul - Sep
Moderate rainfall makes lakes come alive. Lush green landscapes but some outdoor activities may be limited.
