Wayanad, Green Paradise

Where Misty Mountains Meet Lush Forests

22°C • Pleasant

Overview

Wayanad, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a nature lover's paradise with misty mountains, lush forests, spice plantations, and rich tribal heritage. Known as the "Green Paradise of Kerala," Wayanad offers breathtaking landscapes, waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, and ancient caves. The district is part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and is home to indigenous tribal communities. From trekking to Chembra Peak to exploring Edakkal Caves' prehistoric carvings, from wildlife spotting in Tholpetty to relaxing at Pookode Lake, Wayanad provides diverse experiences amidst unspoiled nature.

How to Reach Wayanad

3 hrs from airport

By Air

  • Nearest airport: Calicut (CCJ) - 100km away
  • Other options: Kannur (CNN) - 120km, Bangalore (BLR) - 290km
  • Direct flights from major Indian cities
  • Major airlines: IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet
  • Pre-paid taxis available (₹2500-3500 to Wayanad)
50-54 hrs from Delhi

By Train

  • Nearest major station: Kozhikode (CLT) - 75km
  • Other option: Thalassery (TLY) - 80km
  • Rajdhani Express from Delhi to Kozhikode
  • Other important trains: Mangalore Mail, Kannur Express
  • Taxis/buses available from stations to Wayanad
6-7 hrs from Bangalore

By Road

  • Well-connected via NH766, SH29, SH54
  • Bangalore to Wayanad via Mysore, Bandipur
  • Volvo/AC buses from major cities (₹1200-1800 per person)
  • Private taxi options (₹10-14 per km)
  • Scenic route through Bandipur Tiger Reserve

Must-Visit Attractions in Wayanad

Top Rated

Chembra Peak

Highest peak in Wayanad with a heart-shaped lake en route. Offers challenging trek with breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. Requires forest department permission.

4.9 (3,420 reviews)
500 (Trekking Fee)
Historical

Edakkal Caves

Ancient caves with Neolithic petroglyphs dating back to 6000 BCE. Requires a moderate hike to reach. Features carvings of human figures, animals, and symbols.

4.7 (4,130 reviews)
100 (Indians)
Wildlife

Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary

Part of Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary with jeep safaris to spot elephants, deer, bison, and occasionally tigers and leopards. Best visited early morning.

4.6 (2,810 reviews)
300 (Safari)
Nature

Pookode Lake

Serene freshwater lake surrounded by forests. Offers boating, nature walks, and an aquarium. Home to blue lotus flowers and diverse birdlife.

50 (Entry)

Wayanad's Flavorful Cuisine

Bamboo Chicken

Chicken marinated in spices, stuffed in bamboo stems and cooked over fire, a tribal delicacy with smoky flavor.

Best at: Tribal Village Resorts

Kerala Red Rice Meal

Traditional meal with red rice, sambar, avial, thoran, and pickle, served on banana leaf with local vegetables.

Best at: Udupi restaurants in Kalpetta

Kallumakkaya Curry

Mussels cooked in coconut gravy with traditional spices, a tribal specialty available in season.

Best at: Tribal homestays

Puttu & Kadala

Steamed rice cakes layered with coconut served with black chickpea curry, a popular breakfast dish.

Best at: Local tea shops

Wayanad Coffee

Locally grown coffee known for its rich aroma and flavor. Visit coffee plantations for fresh brews.

Best at: Coffee plantations

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaf and grilled, a signature Kerala dish.

Best at: Lakeside restaurants

Best Time to Visit Wayanad

Winter

15°C - 28°C
Oct - Feb
Pleasant weather perfect for trekking and sightseeing. Peak tourist season with clear skies.

Summer

20°C - 35°C
Mar - May
Warm days but cooler than plains. Good for visiting waterfalls and early morning safaris.

Monsoon

18°C - 25°C
Jun - Sep
Heavy rainfall creates lush landscapes. Fewer tourists but some activities may be restricted.

Shoulder Season

18°C - 30°C
Mar & Sep
Transition periods with moderate weather and fewer crowds. Great for budget travelers.
